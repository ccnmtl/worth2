Facts About HIV and STIs

Women suffer more frequent and severe symptoms of STIs than men.

Some STIs are more likely to be asymptomatic in women than men. STIs in women can be associated with pelvic inflammatory disease, ectopic pregnancy, infertility, and cancer etc. You cannot get an STI from a toilet seat. Most STIs are transmitted by sexual contact only. HSV 1 and 2 can be transmitted through skin to skin contact.

Viral STIs can be treated, but there are not cures for most of them. Once your body is infected with a viral STI, your body will always carry it. HPV (genital warts) causes cervical cancer and affect reproductive health. You should get screened for HPV (genital warts) annually. Women should get an annual exam to receive a Pap test to screen for evidence of HPV (genital warts) infection. HPV (genital warts) is easily transmissible and the most prevalent STI, particularly among young men and women. HPV (genital warts) is VERY Common.

You may be infected and experience no symptoms. In fact, for many STIs such as Chlamydia, most people transmit the infection without experiencing any symptoms. Chlamydia has lasting effects. Chlamydia can lead to pelvic inflammatory disease, ectopic pregnancy, and infertility among women. A mother can pass gonorrhea to her unborn child. Gonorrhea can be passed on to the child during birth. It can cause blindness in the newborn.

You don't have to have an outbreak or other symptoms in order to be infectious and pass HSV-2 (genital herpes) to a partner. HSV-2 (genital herpes) shedding is responsible for genital herpes transmission whether these episodes lead to recognized outbreaks or are clinically asymptomatic. In fact, up to 70% of transmissions can be attributed to asymptomatic shedding.

While there is no cure for HSV-2 (genital herpes) there is an anti-viral treatment that can reduce viral shedding, which reduces risk of transmission to other partners.

Syphilis can cause slow destruction of the heart, brain, nerves, eyes, or joints in 1/3 of infected people. Without antibiotics, death is likely.

Women account for half of the world's cases of HIV. You cannot get HIV by kissing someone who has it. Saliva may contain HIV but not in sufficient quantities to transmit the virus. Even during treatment with the new medications HIV can still progress, and an infected person can transmit the virus to others. Withdrawal, when a man pulls his penis out of a woman before ejaculation, provides no protection against STIs.

Steps for Using a Male Condom

Before Intercourse

  1. Ensure that the condom is made out of latex, polyurethane, or other non-animal based material, and that it is not expired.
  2. Open the package, being careful not to tear the condom.
  3. Proceed to place the condom on the penis, making sure to leave room for the tip.
  4. Slowly unroll the condom all the way down to the base of the penis.
  5. If necessary, use water-based lubricants only (e.g., KY Jelly or ID lube).

Immediately After Ejaculation

  1. Hold the condom at the base of the penis and withdraw before the penis becomes flaccid.
  2. Roll the condom down and remove it from the penis, being careful not to spill contents.
  3. Dispose of the condom in a trashcan.

Dental Dams

Dental dams are small, thin, square pieces of latex that are used for oral-vaginal or oral-anal sex. Dental dams help reduce the spread of STIs during oral sex by putting a barrier between the mouth and any vaginal and anal fluids that contain bacteria and viruses. They come in a variety of sizes and flavors so you can find a dam that satisfies your needs. Dental dams prevent many STIs, such as herpes, genital warts and HIV, that can be transmitted through oral sex. Like condoms, dental dams must be used correctly and consistently in order to be effective.

How To Use a Dental Dam Properly

  • Use a new dental dam for each sexual activity.
  • Make sure there are no holes or tears in the dental dam. Check the dnetal dam by holding it up to light or check to see if any water can go through it.
  • Do not use any oil based lubricants such as Vaseline, petroleum jelly, baby oil, or lotions. Oil based lubricants can break down the materical of the dental dam, causing it to leak or tear.
  • Hold the dental dam over the vagina or anus during oral sex. Make sure that the dental dam stays in place. Only use one side of the dental dam and never share the same dental dam with your partner.
  • Store the dental dam in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Heat and sunlight can breakdown latex which can cause the dental dam to tear.

Steps for using a Female Condom

Before Intercourse

  1. Ensure that the condom has not expired
  2. Squeeze package to ensure that there are no holes/tears
  3. Open package, being careful not to tear the condom
  4. Place water-based lubricant on the outside of the condom to ensure comfortable insertion
  5. Find a comfortable position to insert the condom
  6. Squeeze the sides of the inner ring together
  7. Insert the inner ring and condom into your vagina
  8. Push the inner ring into the vagina until it reaches the cervix

After Intercourse

  1. Squeeze and twist the outer ring
  2. Gently remove the condom from your vagina or anus
  3. Throw the condom away in trashcan
